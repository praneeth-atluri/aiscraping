Q&A
 
What is Scholarcy?
Scholarcy is an AI-powered online tool that specializes in summarizing and evaluating academic research. It serves as a convenient platform for rapidly assessing the relevance and importance of documents such as articles, reports, and book chapters.
 
What does Scholarcy do?
Scholarcy converts long text documents into summary flashcards. It emphasizes key points, assesses significant data, and offers automatic reference extraction. It can also extract tables, figures, and images. Additionally, it links to open-access sources and integrates with open-access repositories.
 
Who is Scholarcy for?
Scholarcy targets a range of users including researchers, students, journalists, librarians, and anyone in need of quickly digesting a large amount of information.
 
How does Scholarcy evaluate articles?
Scholarcy evaluates articles by breaking them down into digestible sections. It locates crucial information in the documents such as main findings, data analyses, study participants, and limitations, helping users to quickly determine the document's importance to their work.
 
What types of documents can Scholarcy summarize?
Scholarcy is capable of summarizing different types of documents including research articles, reports, and book chapters. It can process both Word and PDF formats.
 
How does Scholarcy extract references?
Scholarcy extracts references automatically. The system is designed to identify and extract citation information from the documents it processes and provide links to the open-access versions of cited sources.
 
What does the Scholarcy flashcard contain?
A Scholarcy flashcard is a compressed summary of an article, report, or document. It highlights the key facts, figures, and references from the document and presents them in an easy-to-understand format.
 
What is the Scholarcy Chrome and Edge extension?
The Scholarcy Chrome and Edge extension is an add-on that directly integrates Scholarcy with your browser. The extension works with open-access repositories, building a searchable collection of summary cards that are accessible from any device.
 
How does Scholarcy work with open-access repositories?
Scholarcy works with open-access repositories like arXiv, biorXiv, and OSF Preprints through its browser extension. It scans these platforms and summarizes the documents, creating flashcards that can be accessed and searched from any device.
 
Can Scholarcy be used on multiple devices?
Yes, Scholarcy can be used on multiple devices. Once summary flashcards are generated, they can be accessed and managed from any device via the Scholarcy Library, a paid monthly subscription service.
 
How does Scholarcy help students, journalists, and researchers?
Through its AI-powered summarizing feature, Scholarcy assists students, journalists, and researchers in efficiently digesting and understanding large volumes of documents. It reduces the time spent on sifting through documents by over 70% by breaking down articles into key sections for quicker appraisal.
 
What is the significance of the Scholarcy Library?
The Scholarcy Library is a paid subscription service that builds a personal collection of summarized research stored as flashcards. This library is searchable and accessible from any device, making it easy to manage, access, and review summaries of numerous documents.
 
How does Scholarcy extract figures, tables, and images?
Scholarcy is equipped with feature extraction functionality that allows it to identify and pull figures, tables, and images from the documents it processes. This information is included in the summary flashcards to provide a more detailed and comprehensive summary.
 
How to sign up for Scholarcy?
Signing up for Scholarcy can be done on their website. There is a 'Start free' option to commence with a free trial. A paid subscription gives access to the Scholarcy Library and all its features.
 
What type of files can Scholarcy process?
Scholarcy supports the summarization of both Word and PDF documents.
 
What are the key features of Scholarcy?
Key features of Scholarcy include its ability to summarize long articles, automatic extraction of references, linking to open-access sources, figure/table/image extraction, and the creation of summary flashcards. It also offers a browser extension that works with open-access repositories.

How does Scholarcy build a summarized research library?
Scholarcy builds a summarized research library by creating flashcards for each article, report, or document processed. Using its browser extension, Scholarcy also integrates with open-access repositories such as arXiv, biorXiv, and OSF Preprints, further expanding the user's library with summaries from these platforms.
 
What open access sources does Scholarcy link to?
Scholarcy's system recognizes and links to open-access versions of cited sources. It works with repositories such as arXiv, biorXiv, and OSF Preprints to provide accessible sourcing for references.
 
How does Scholarcy help to speed-read an article?
Scholarcy enables users to speed-read articles by breaking them down into key sections, extracting the most essential facts, figures, and references. It simplifies the document into bite-sized segments, helping users understand the main points in less time.
